## Classification and data outline

1. Physical both derived and base units
    1. metric system - SI units (Also add units as short form)
    2. Imperial system - (feet, yard, lbs)

2. Monetary or economic units

3. Simple count
    1. Numerical
        1. Numbers and decimals

        2. Ratios and fractions
            
            ex:- 1/2, 3 out of 5
            
        3. Sentences with mixed forms
            
            ex:- Two people bought 10kg of flour
            
        4. International and South Asian (sentences containing both type)
            
            ex:- Million, Lakhs
        
        5. Alphanumeric values
            ex:- 1st, 2nd, 3rd etc.
            
    2. Informal methods (dozen, score, teaspoon, cup, pinch)

4. Ambiguous units
    
    ex:- Ton (2000 pounds in US and 1000kg in metric), pounds (weight and currency)
    
5. Dates and temporal measurement
    1. Absolute
        
        ex:- 2/2/2025, 6 September, seconds, minutes, week
        
    2. Relative
        
        ex:- yesterday, day before yesterday, day after tomorrow, yesteryear
        
6. Prefixes (deca, mega, giga) (This)

7. Approximate quantifiers
    
    ex:- few, many, several, plenty
    
8. Human-centric units
    
    ex:- blink of an eye, arms length, knee high, waist high
